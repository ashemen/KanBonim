# Sitemap, Strategy, And Page Structure

## Strategy Summary

The rebuilt website should feel like a professional home base for Kan Bonim: warm, serious, culturally sensitive, practical, and easy to act on.

The visitor should immediately understand:

- Kan Bonim helps build lasting Jewish homes through matchmaking, coaching, workshops, lectures, and community matchmaker training.
- The work is professional, personal, discreet, and grounded in the religious-Zionist world.
- Ariel and Nechama Shemen bring education, coaching, matchmaking experience, research, and family/community sensitivity.
- There is a clear next step for each audience.

## Page Goals

### Homepage

Audience:

- Singles, parents, friends, community leaders, educators, rabbis, and organizations.

Question answered:

- Who are Kan Bonim, what do they offer, and why should I trust them?

Action:

- Choose a service path or start a conversation.

### Services Overview

Audience:

- Visitors who know they need help but do not yet know which service fits.

Question answered:

- Which kind of support is right for me or my community?

Action:

- Open a service page or contact for guidance.

### Effective Matchmaking

Audience:

- Religious-Zionist singles and families seeking serious, guided matchmaking.

Question answered:

- How is this different from casual suggestions or a passive database?

Action:

- Request an introductory call or begin intake.

### Coaching

Audience:

- Singles who feel stuck, confused, tired, hesitant, or unclear in dating.

Question answered:

- Can personal coaching help me approach relationships with more clarity and readiness?

Action:

- Book an introductory coaching conversation.

### Workshops

Audience:

- Singles, communities, institutions, groups.

Question answered:

- What practical tools can participants gain in a safe group setting?

Action:

- Register for a workshop or invite Kan Bonim to run one.

### Lectures

Audience:

- Communities, schools, yeshivot, midrashot, rabbis, educators, parents, conferences.

Question answered:

- What can Kan Bonim teach our audience about dating, choice, and relationships today?

Action:

- Invite a lecture.

### Community Matchmaker Training

Audience:

- Informal matchmakers, community leaders, friends, neighbors, parents, and anyone trying to help others build a home.

Question answered:

- How can people with good intentions learn to help wisely and responsibly?

Action:

- Join a cohort or invite training for a community.

### Resources

Audience:

- Visitors seeking depth, thought leadership, podcast episodes, and articles.

Question answered:

- What does Kan Bonim think and teach about modern dating, religious-Zionist singles, and matchmaking?

Action:

- Read/listen, then contact or explore a related service.

### About

Audience:

- Anyone checking trust, credentials, personality, and fit.

Question answered:

- Who are Ariel and Nechama, and why are they credible?

Action:

- Contact or choose a service.

### Contact

Audience:

- Visitors ready to ask a question or begin.

Question answered:

- How do I reach Kan Bonim, and what details should I provide?

Action:

- WhatsApp, phone, email, or form submission.

## Homepage Structure

1. Hero
   - H1: כאן בונים
   - Supporting line: שידוכים, אימון וליווי לבניית קשר זוגי מתוך מודעות, רגישות ואחריות.
   - Primary CTA: לשיחת היכרות
   - Secondary CTA: הכירו את השירותים

2. Problem / belief section
   - Short version of the current homepage idea: a good match does not just happen; it is built.

3. Service pathways
   - Five service cards with audience labels and clear CTAs.

4. How the process feels
   - Personal, discreet, professional, culturally sensitive, practical.

5. About Ariel and Nechama
   - Photo, two short bios, link to full about page.

6. Testimonials
   - 2-3 polished quote cards.

7. Resources / podcast highlight
   - Latest articles and podcast episodes.

8. Community / survey / timely initiative
   - Only if current and active.

9. Contact CTA
   - Phone, WhatsApp, email, form prompt.

## Service Page Template

Each service page should use the same structure:

1. Hero
   - Service name
   - Short audience-focused summary
   - CTA

2. Who this is for
   - Specific visitor types and situations.

3. What we do
   - Clear explanation of the service.

4. How it works
   - 3-5 process steps.

5. What you can gain
   - Outcomes, not guarantees.

6. Why Kan Bonim
   - Relevant credentials and experience.

7. FAQ
   - Confidentiality, format, audience, location/Zoom, next step.

8. CTA
   - Specific to service.

## SEO Strategy

### URL Strategy

Use English route slugs for technical stability and shareability, while Hebrew page content and metadata remain Hebrew.

Example:

- `/services/effective-matchmaking`
- `/services/coaching`
- `/resources/singlehood-snapshot`

### Internal Linking

- Homepage links to every service.
- Every service links to contact and 1-2 relevant resources.
- Articles link to relevant services.
- About links to services and contact.
- Footer repeats all core service links.

### Metadata Examples

Homepage title:

- כאן בונים | שדכנות, אימון וליווי זוגי לציבור הדתי-לאומי

Homepage description:

- שדכנות אפקטיבית, אימון לרווקים ורווקות, סדנאות, הרצאות והכשרת שדכנים בקהילה מתוך מקצועיות, רגישות ואחריות.

## Review Before Design

- Confirm whether `/about` and `/contact` should be separate pages or sections on the homepage.
- Confirm whether English route slugs are acceptable.
- Confirm primary CTA: WhatsApp, phone, or form.
- Confirm whether the survey is active enough to include.
- Confirm whether resources should combine articles, videos, and podcast or split podcast separately.
